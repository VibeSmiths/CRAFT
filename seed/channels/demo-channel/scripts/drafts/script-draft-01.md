*[leans into camera, dead serious]*

—trust a developer who says "it works on my machine."

**Five git commands.** That's it. Five commands that separate the "I just started coding" crowd from the "I've seen things" veterans.

*[holds up one finger]*

**Number one: `git stash`**. Your PM walks in with an "urgent fix." You're mid-feature. Instead of panic-committing garbage, you stash it. Clean slate. Fix the bug. Pop your stash. Chef's kiss.

*[two fingers]*

**`git bisect`.** Something broke. You don't know when. Bisect does a binary search through your commits to find the exact one that ruined everything. Like a detective, but useful.

*[three fingers, speeding up]*

**`git reflog`.** You did a hard reset and lost everything? No you didn't. Reflog remembers what you forgot. It's your git safety net.

*[four fingers]*

**`git cherry-pick`.** Grab one specific commit from another branch. Just that one. No merge. No drama.

*[five fingers, intense]*

**`git worktree`.** Run two branches simultaneously in separate folders. Review a PR while your feature branch keeps cooking.

*[beat]*

Five commands. Zero excuses. And the next time someone says they "lost their code" — you should never—
